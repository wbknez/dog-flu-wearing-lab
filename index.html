Exploring the Transmission Dynamics of Canine Influenza Virus

Will Knez | Wearing Lab | Nov 12, 2021

Background

Human Pandemics from Host Range Shifts

  • SARS (palm civets, 2003)
  • H1N1 (swine, 2009)
  • MERs-COV (bats and camels, 2014)
  • COVID (more bats, 2019)

Methods

Phylogenetic Analysis

  • HA1, M, and NP gene sequences
  • Uses data from 12 states
  • Clustering analysis (Association and Parsimony Index)
  • Estimates national \(R_e\) using MCMC model

Epidemic Model

\[\begin{aligned} \dot{S} & = \mu - (\lambda + \alpha + \delta)S\\ \dot{I} & = \lambda S - (\gamma + \delta)I\\ \dot{R} & = \gamma(I + W) - \delta R\\ \dot{V} & = \alpha S - (\epsilon \lambda + \delta)V\\ \dot{W} & = \epsilon \lambda V - (\gamma + \delta)W\\ \end{aligned} \] \[\begin{aligned} \lambda & = \frac{\beta(I + \omega W)}{N} \end{aligned} \]

Control Strategies

  • Representative "vaccination" program
  • Models viral load, not symptoms
  • Not 100% efficacious
  • Reduces disease spread proportional to vaccine coverage

Metapopulation Model

  • Expands single-shelter to a network
  • Estimates parameters from data
  • Shelters vary by size, intake rate, and output rate
